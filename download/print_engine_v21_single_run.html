⚙ The Single-Run "One-Plate" Strategy
Simplified workflow: one setup, one run, one wash-up. The overages on smaller orders offset the press-time savings.
1
Grid Layout
6×3 rigid grid on a 24"×18" sheet. Each cell = 9.39cm × 12.03cm. Total: 18 outs per sheet.
2
Assign Outs
G=6, F=3, E=3, D=2, C=2, B=1, A=1. Every cell is the same size — standard die-cutting chase.
3
Magic Number
Run 1,144 sheets to clear all minimums. A needs 825 ÷ 1 = 825 min; B needs 924 ÷ 1 = 924. The maximum wins = 1,144.
4
Ship It
One CMYK plate set, one press run, one die-cut pass. Overruns on C and A are the trade-off for simplicity.

★ Press Specs & Sticker Pro-Tips
Everything the press operator and die-cutter need to know at a glance.
P Press Specs
Sheet: 24.0" × 18.0" (60.96cm × 45.72cm)
Run Length: 1,144 sheets
Paper Order: 1,250 sheets (setup + 9.3% spoilage)
Plates: 1 set CMYK
Margins: 0.25cm sides, 0.3cm top/bottom
Cell Size: 9.39cm × 12.03cm
B Bleed Requirements
Sticker Size: 3.5" × 4.5"
Bleed: Extend background color/art at least 2mm beyond the die-line on all sides.
This ensures no white edges appear after the die-cutter makes its cut. The orange dashed lines in the SVG show this bleed boundary.
D Die-Cutting Tool
Since every position on the sheet is the same size (9.39cm × 12.03cm cell), you can use a standard uniform die-cutting chase. No custom rules needed — every cell takes the same die. This is a major cost and time advantage.
G Grain Direction
Keep the paper grain parallel to the 24" side (the long dimension). This prevents the sticker backing from curling after individual units are cut apart. Grain-perpendicular cuts cause the backing to warp and stickers to peel prematurely.
⚠ Strategy Trade-Off: Single-Run vs. Two-Plate
Why choose one over the other?

Single-Run (This Plan)

  • 1,144 sheets vs. 1,087 sheets (two-plate)
  • +57 more sheets, but only one setup
  • One plate set = lower plate cost
  • One wash-up = less downtime
  • More overruns (especially C +614, A +319)
  • Best for: rush jobs, simpler workflow

Two-Plate (v20 Plan)

  • 1,087 sheets total (856 + 231)
  • 57 fewer sheets, but two setups
  • Two plate sets = higher plate cost
  • Two wash-ups = more downtime
  • Tighter overruns (max +564 on F)
  • Best for: paper-sensitive jobs, large volumes
